### **1. Heredity vs. Environment**
- **Heredity**: Traits inherited via genes (e.g., eye color, height).
- **Environment**: Traits influenced by surroundings (e.g., language, skills).
- **Interaction**: Some traits (e.g., height) depend on both genetics and environment (nutrition).

### **2. Cells and Chromosomes**
- **Somatic Cells**: 46 chromosomes (23 pairs).
- **Gametes**: 23 chromosomes (haploid; 23 from each parent).
- **DNA & Genes**: Chromosomes contain DNA; genes are DNA segments controlling traits.
- **Sex Determination**: XX (female), XY (male).

### **3. DNA and Mutations**
- **Structure**: Nitrogen bases (A, T, C, G) in sequence.
- **Mutations**: Changes in DNA (e.g., deletion, substitution).
  - **Causes**: Radiation, replication errors.
  - **Effects**: Harmful (cancer), neutral, or beneficial (survival advantage).
  - **Inheritance**: Only if mutation occurs in gametes.

### **4. Cell Division**
- **Mitosis**: Produces 2 identical diploid cells for growth/repair.
- **Meiosis**: Produces 4 haploid gametes with genetic diversity (crossing over).

### **5. Twins**
- **Identical (Monozygotic)**: One zygote splits; same DNA/sex.
- **Fraternal (Dizygotic)**: Two eggs fertilized; genetically like siblings.

### **6. Inheritance Patterns**
- **Dominant (B)** vs. **Recessive (b)**: Brown eyes (B) dominant over blue (b).
- **Punnett Squares**: Predict offspring genotypes (e.g., Bb Ã— Bb â†’ 25% bb).

### **7. Biotechnology & GMOs**
- **Selective Breeding**: Choosing traits over generations (e.g., disease-resistant crops).
- **Genetic Engineering**: Direct DNA modification (e.g., CRISPR).
  - **GMO Benefits**: Pest resistance, enhanced nutrition (golden rice).
  - **Risks**: Ecosystem disruption, reduced biodiversity.

- **Genetics**: Study of heredity.
- **Gene Therapy**: Replacing faulty genes (e.g., cystic fibrosis).
- **CRISPR**: Gene-editing tool.
